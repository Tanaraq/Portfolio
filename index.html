About Me

Hi! My name is Annabel Kooistra, and I'm an aspiring Web Developer.

I started coding to self-educate and to explore the options of a careerswitch. Currently, I've almost completed the Front-End Engineer career path on Codecademy, to be followed by the Full-Stack Engineer path.

What I like most about coding is that it it combines logical thinking with problem solving and creativity. And there's so much to discover, that I can literally keep learning my entire life!

I'm a quiet yet enthousiastic person, intelligent and curious by nature. In my last job, I used to be a salesperson, practising my communicative and problem-solving skills on a daily basis. A large part of my job entailed being a mentor and coach to my colleagues. I also gave workshops and trainings, lifting all our communicative skills to a higher level. Since I have a great affinity and lots of experience with retail and customer service, I'm intrigued by websites that strive to offer the best customer experience possible.

In my freetime I like to enjoy the outdoors, or be creative in any way. I like to start my day with
yoga. No, not because I wanna be a 'yoga-girl'!
It's more practical: when I start my day with yoga, I notice that I'm more relaxed and more focused during the day. And it's a nice counterpart to sitting behind a laptop a lot...
I'm creative and curious, always interested in how things are made. The result is that I'm collecting
hobbies. Yes, before I discovered coding I had tons of other hobbies!
Ranging from exploring ancient handicraft-techniques (like tablet-weaving or nalbinding), to making my own clothes, or my own wine, or growing vegetables (especially the ones totally unsuitable for the dutch climate).
I also love repairing stuff like camping-equipment, my bike or the coffeemachine...
Some hobbies turned out to be very useful for web development! For instance, painting gave me a deep understanding of color-theory, while practicing calligraphy taught me a lot about typography and layouts.

Projects

Todays Dashboard

Description:

I like to start my day with a cup of coffee, contemplating what today will bring and making a list with my chores and goals for that day. This personal dashboard app features a header with day,date and birthday-notifications, a weatherwidget, an inspirational quote and a taskboard. The taskboard has several categories, each task can be made recurring. Tasks that are recurring today, will show up on the front tab, called "Today"!

Libraries used:

React, React-Redux, Redux-Persist.

What I learned from this project:

Great practice with a more advanced react-redux-app of my own, handling multiple features/slices and multiple APIs.

Jammming

Description:

Jammming is a React-web-app that allows users to search the Spotify library, create a custom playlist, then save it to their Spotify account. This project was a Practice Project from the Codecademy Career Path to Front-End Engineer.

Techniques used:

HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React JS

What I learned from this project:

How to build a React-app using React components, passing state, and making HTTP-requests to the Spotify API.
